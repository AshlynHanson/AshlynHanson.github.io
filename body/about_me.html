About Me

Software Development Engineer

I'm currently a Junior at Truman State University majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Design. Typically, I'm located in Kansas City, MO unless I'm on campus in Kirksville, MO. Some of the classes I've taken include, Object-Oriented Programming, Data Structures and Algorithms, Programming Languages, and Intro to BioInformatics. I am the Risk Manager for the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) at Truman, where I was a team leader for the fullstack team on the Web Development team. We worked with a non-profit to create a revitalized website for the city of Kirksville. I am also a Teaching Assistant for one of Truman's Computer Science Professors. Some of the classes that I work with include, Computer Science I and Artificial Intelligence Seminar.

For the past two summers, I interned at Amazon AWS where I worked on the Braket Quantum Computing Team as an SDE intern. My first summer, in 2020, I worked closely with another intern to create a Machine Learning model to predict the execution duration of a Quantum Circuit. We also created a function that retrieved previously executed Quantum circuits and parsed out the necessary information for our data set. My second summer, in 2021, I simplified and improved the operations for the team. I implemented a program that automated a manual on-call task and cut the time for on-call to respond to a customer issue down from days to minutes. Both of these projects were created using AWS Serverless Architecture including, AWS Lambda functions, S3 Buckets, DynamoDB tables, and CloudFormation. They also included unit and integration tests and metrics to ensure the programs work as expected.
